## What's a JAMstack? ğŸ¤”

- JavaScript
- APIs
- Markup

</Split>


<SpeakerNotes>
  <p>- The JAMstack is not about specific technologies.</p>
  <p>- LAMP, MERN, whatever</p>
  <p>- It's more a paradigm of how to deploy</p>
  <p>- a way of building websites and apps that helps deliver:</p>
  <p>- better performance,</p>
  <p>- higher security,</p>
  <p>- lower cost of scaling,</p>
  <p>- and a better developer experience.</p>
</SpeakerNotes>

---

## JavaScript

<SpeakerNotes>
  <p>
    - Any dynamic programming during the request/response cycle is
    handled by JavaScript,
  </p>
  <p>- running entirely on the client (the browser). </p>
  <p>
    - This could be any frontend framework, library, or even vanilla
    JavaScript.
  </p>
</SpeakerNotes>

---

## APIs

<SpeakerNotes>
  <p>
    - All server-side processes or database actions are abstracted
    into reusable APIs,
  </p>
  <p>- accessed over HTTPS with JavaScript.</p>
  <p>- These can be custom-built or leverage third-party services.</p>
</SpeakerNotes>

---

## Markup

<SpeakerNotes>
  <p>- Templated markup should be prebuilt at deploy time,</p>
  <p>- usually using a site generator for content sites,</p>
  <p>- or a build tool for web apps.</p>
</SpeakerNotes>
